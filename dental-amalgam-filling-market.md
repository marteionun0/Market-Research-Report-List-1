<p><p>The dental amalgam filling market is primarily categorized into low-copper and high-copper amalgams. Low-copper amalgam, containing about 3-6% copper, is known for its strength but is prone to corrosion and marginal breakdown over time. In contrast, high-copper amalgam, which contains 10-30% copper, offers enhanced properties such as reduced corrosion, improved durability, and better biocompatibility. This shift to high-copper amalgams reflects advancements in dental materials, influencing market trends and consumer preferences in restorative dentistry.</p></p>
