<p><p>The industrial wood adhesives market is primarily categorized into natural and synthetic types. Natural adhesives, derived from renewable sources such as starch, protein, and resins, are environmentally friendly and often used in eco-conscious applications. Synthetic adhesives, on the other hand, are chemically engineered products, including polyvinyl acetate (PVA) and polyurethane, known for their strong bonding capabilities and durability. These synthetic options are widely used in high-performance applications due to their resistance to moisture, temperature variations, and overall longevity.</p></p>
