<p><p>Keypad electronic access control systems can be categorized into networked and standalone systems. Networked systems connect to a centralized management system, allowing multiple entry points to be monitored and managed remotely. They offer real-time data and access control across various locations, ideal for businesses with extensive security needs. In contrast, standalone systems operate independently, managing access at a single entry point without requiring a connection to other devices. They are simpler and cost-effective, suited for smaller applications or limited access areas.</p></p>
