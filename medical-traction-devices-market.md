<p><p>The medical traction devices market consists of two primary types: skeletal traction devices and skin traction devices. Skeletal traction devices involve the use of pins or wires inserted into the bone to apply steady pull on the skeletal system, facilitating alignment and healing of fractures. In contrast, skin traction devices utilize adhesive straps or harnesses applied to the skin to achieve traction, primarily for managing musculoskeletal conditions. Both types are essential for patient recovery and rehabilitation in orthopedic settings.</p></p>
