<p><p>The optical microscope immersion oil market is divided into two main types: synthetic immersion oils and natural immersion oils. Synthetic immersion oils are man-made and often characterized by consistent refractive indices, minimal viscosity changes, and resistance to degradation, making them suitable for high-performance applications. In contrast, natural immersion oils are derived from organic sources, traditionally used in microscopy, but may exhibit variability in quality and refractive properties. Both types are essential for enhancing image clarity and detail in optical microscopy.</p></p>
